About Me

Hi, I'm Eshan Khan, a 2nd year Mechatronics Engineering student who enjoys turning coffee into CAD models and chaos into functioning (mostly) mechanisms. I’ve always been drawn to solving tough problems—whether it’s designing a part that won’t explode under load or figuring out why my SolidWorks assembly just turned itself into a modern art sculpture. I’ve always had a blast mixing hands-on work with brain-bending logic puzzles.

When I’m not sketching in AutoCAD or trying to remember why I created that weird constraint, you can find me at the gym. It’s the one place where gains are linear and bugs don’t exist (unless you count dropping a dumbbell on your foot). I also enjoy watching sci-fi movies and mentally critiquing their engineering accuracy—because who really believes a spaceship can turn like a racecar?

Professionally, I’ve worked on firmware for embedded devices and led tutoring sessions where I explained math, physics, and how not to throw your calculator out the window. These experiences taught me to communicate clearly, adapt quickly, and stay calm when technology decides to do its own thing.

In addition to CAD and hardware, I also enjoy coding, especially when it works on the first try (a rare but glorious event). I've worked with C/C++ for embedded systems, Python for data analysis and automation, and MATLAB when I feel like wrestling equations into submission. Whether it’s debugging a sensor interface or building scripts to speed up workflows, I love the feeling of getting a stubborn block of code to finally behave. You can check out my work on my github.

I'm always looking for my next challenge, whether it’s a cool internship, a design problem, or just a debate over whether AutoCAD or SolidWorks is more stubborn. Let’s connect and build something awesome (or at least get it to compile).

Impact Reducing Bumper System

What

  • Designed and implemented a bumper system to reduce the impact of a collision by 50% while enhancing energy dissipation and structural integrity.
  • Developed a cost-effective solution using popsicle sticks and rubber bands to minimize object deformation and improve impact absorption in low-speed accidents

How

  • Applied engineering skills and GD&T principles to design and optimize the bumper system for maximum energy dissipation and structural integrity.
  • Developed a MATLAB simulation to calculate acceleration and velocity during multiple rebounds, analyzing real-time performance data to refine the bumper design.

Results

Achieved a 50% reduction in collision impact by optimizing the bumper system design for energy dissipation and structural integrity, enhancing safety and minimizing vehicle damage in low-speed accidents.
